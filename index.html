Welcome to the Supyire Lexicography Project

The project preserves and documents Supyire ‚Äî producing bilingual dictionaries, a digitized text corpus, training team members, and delivering a smartphone app to make this knowledge broadly accessible.

About the Project

The Supyire Lexicography Project builds on decades of research by Prof. Robert Carlson and collaborators, aiming to produce Supyire‚ÄìFrench and Supyire‚ÄìEnglish dictionaries, a comprehensive online text corpus, and mobile tools for educators, translators, and researchers.

The project is presented to the Department of Language, Linguistics, and Translation at Africa International University. It seeks to make existing lexical and textual data accessible to Supyire communities and to scholars worldwide.

History of Research on Supyire

Research began in the late 1970s under SIL International, led by Robert Carlson. Since then lexical databases and text corpora were compiled and improved alongside Supyire speakers and collaborators. The New Testament (2008) and ongoing translation work are part of the project's linguistic context.

Current Toolbox Database

The Toolbox database contains lexical entries, interlinear examples, and texts used to fuel the dictionary and corpus work. We are standardizing entries and preparing export to FLEx.

Project Goals

  • Produce Supyire‚ÄìFrench and Supyire‚ÄìEnglish dictionaries (print & digital).
  • Create a smartphone dictionary app.
  • Develop and publish an online Supyire text database.
  • Standardize Toolbox data and transfer to FLEx.

Project Activities

Dictionary Production

  • Standardize entries, add orthography and example sentences, and correct translations.
  • Output print and electronic editions and prepare app-ready data exports.

Text Database

  • Revise transcription, interlinearize, translate, and annotate texts for online publication.
  • Publish the annotated text database and integrate with dictionary resources.

Operational Procedures

  • The project is run under AIU's Department of Language, Linguistics, and Translation (LLTD).
  • Quarterly reports (end of March, June, September, December) to the Head of Department.
  • Funding from individual and institutional donations; AIU Finance approves disbursements.
  • AIU and LLTD will be acknowledged in all publications.

Risks & Mitigation

  • Training dependency: Project success depends on RS and MO completing training ‚Äî RC will mentor and coordinate training.
  • Travel limitations: Political issues in Mali may restrict travel; meetings will be held in neighboring countries if necessary.
  • Leadership transition: Due to RC's age, responsibilities will be transferred progressively to RS and MO.
